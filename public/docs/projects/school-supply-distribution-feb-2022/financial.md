## 5. Variance Analysis

Total project expenditure was 20,000 CFA ($33.33 USD) under budget, representing a 6.7% savings. The following factors contributed to the positive variance:

### 5.1 Plastic Buckets (−6,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 3,000 CFA per bucket based on retail pricing. The logistics team identified a wholesale vendor at Buea Main Market who offered a bulk rate of 2,700 CFA per bucket for a purchase of 20 units. The 300 CFA per-unit saving across 20 buckets yielded a total savings of 6,000 CFA.

### 5.2 Plates, Cups & Utensils (−8,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 5,000 CFA per complete set based on purchasing individual items from different vendors. By sourcing all 20 sets from a single kitchenware vendor, the team negotiated a bulk price of 4,600 CFA per set. The vendor offered the discount in exchange for the large single purchase, saving 400 CFA per set across 20 units (8,000 CFA total).

### 5.3 Refreshments (−1,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 15,000 CFA for drinks and snacks for 20 children. Actual cost was 14,000 CFA due to a slight discount on bulk biscuit purchase. The savings was minor but reflects the team's consistent effort to maximize value.

### 5.4 Documentation & Printing (−1,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 5,000 CFA for beneficiary forms, event materials, and printing. Actual cost was 4,000 CFA as fewer forms were needed than anticipated (some documentation was handled digitally via phone).

### 5.5 Contingency (−4,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 10,000 CFA for unforeseen expenses. Actual contingency spend was 6,000 CFA, covering water sachets for volunteers during the event and minor supplies (tape, markers for labeling). The event proceeded smoothly without significant unexpected costs.

### 5.6 Conclusion

The 6.7% under-spend reflects effective procurement practices (wholesale purchasing, vendor negotiation) and smooth event execution that minimized unforeseen costs. All 20 beneficiary children received complete, high-quality supply kits as planned. No planned activities were reduced or eliminated to achieve the savings.

## 6. Financial Controls

### 6.1 Fund Management Procedures

| Control | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Custodian** | Designated Project Treasurer responsible for all cash holdings |
| **Storage** | Funds kept in locked container at Treasurer's residence until disbursement |
| **Contributor receipts** | Written receipts issued to all donors upon contribution |
| **Disbursement** | Cash disbursed only against approved budget lines with verbal authorization from Project Coordinator |
| **Dual Authorization** | All expenditures above 20,000 CFA required approval from both Project Coordinator and Treasurer |
| **Receipt Collection** | Physical receipts obtained for all market purchases |
| **Record Keeping** | All transactions recorded in project cash book on date of expenditure |
| **Reconciliation** | End-of-project reconciliation conducted comparing cash book to receipts |

### 6.2 Improvements Over Previous Project

Compared to ADA/2021/001, the following financial management improvements were implemented:

| Improvement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Contributor receipts** | All donors received written acknowledgment of their contribution (new) |
| **Dual authorization threshold** | Established 20,000 CFA threshold for dual approval (new) |
| **End-of-project reconciliation** | Formal comparison of records to receipts conducted (new) |
| **Budget tracking** | Expenditures tracked against budget lines in real-time during procurement (improved) |
| **Vendor comparison** | Multiple vendors consulted before purchase decisions (improved) |

### 6.3 Limitations Acknowledged

- All transactions remain cash-based (ADA bank account not yet established)
- No formal procurement policy exists (vendor selection based on price comparison and availability)
- No external audit conducted (project size does not yet warrant external review)
- Some small purchases (water sachets, minor supplies) lack formal receipts; handwritten notes recorded

### 6.4 Planned Improvements for Future Projects

- Open dedicated ADA bank account for fund management and donor transparency
- Develop written procurement policy for purchases above 50,000 CFA
- Implement digital record-keeping (spreadsheet) alongside physical cash book
- Photograph all receipts for digital backup and remote access
- Engage independent reviewer for projects exceeding 1,000,000 CFA
- Develop formal donor reporting template for external contributors

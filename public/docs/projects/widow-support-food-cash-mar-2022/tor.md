## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) works to empower vulnerable communities in Cameroon through faith-based programs. Widows in Buea face significant economic hardship, often lacking access to basic food supplies, household essentials, and financial resources. This project represents ADA's most comprehensive single-day outreach, combining material aid with financial assistance and emotional support to establish a multi-pronged intervention model.

## 2. Objectives

1. Provide immediate food security to 15 identified widows through distribution of rice and cooking oil.
2. Supply essential household items (handbags, plastic buckets with supplies) to improve daily living conditions.
3. Deliver direct financial assistance via cash envelopes to address individual urgent needs.
4. Foster community connection and emotional support through a gathering with prayer and companionship.
5. Establish ADA's multi-pronged outreach model combining material, financial, and emotional support.

## 3. Scope of Activities

### 3.1 Community Gathering
- Organize a central gathering venue in Buea for all 15 beneficiaries.
- Facilitate prayer, fellowship, and emotional support.
- Distribute aid packages in a dignified community setting.

### 3.2 Individual Street Visits
- Conduct door-to-door visits for widows unable to attend the gathering.
- Deliver aid packages directly to beneficiaries' homes.
- Provide one-on-one companionship and encouragement.

### 3.3 Aid Distribution
Each beneficiary receives:
- 1 bag of rice (25kg Thai rice)
- 1 bottle of cooking oil (5L)
- 1 new handbag
- 1 plastic bucket with supplies
- 1 cash envelope (3,000 CFA)

## 4. Target Beneficiaries

15 widows identified in Buea, South-West Region, selected based on:
- Verified widow status
- Demonstrated economic vulnerability
- Residence in the Buea community

## 5. Implementation Team

8 volunteers responsible for:
- Beneficiary identification and verification
- Procurement of supplies
- Venue setup and logistics
- Distribution coordination
- Individual home visits
- Documentation and reporting

## 7. Expected Outcomes

- 15 widows receive comprehensive support packages (food + essentials + cash).
- Community gathering strengthens social bonds and provides emotional support.
- ADA's multi-pronged outreach model is validated for future projects.
- Beneficiaries experience improved food security for approximately one month.

# Project Report: Emergency Food Relief - Bamenda Crisis

**Reference:** ADA/2024/001
**Organization:** Apoti Development Association (ADA)
**Period:** January 15 – April 15, 2024
**Location:** Bamenda, North-West Region, Cameroon
**Report Date:** April 2024

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Emergency Food Relief project successfully provided 3 months of sustained food support to 50 internally displaced families (150+ individuals) in Bamenda. Working in coordination with 3 local church partners, ADA delivered monthly food packages from January to March 2024, completing all planned distributions on schedule and under budget.

## 2. Context

The Anglophone crisis continues to force families from rural communities in the North-West and South-West regions into Bamenda. These families arrive with no possessions, no income, and their children unable to attend school. Local churches serve as the first point of contact, but lack resources to provide sustained food assistance. ADA intervened to bridge this gap with a 3-month emergency food relief program.

## 3. Activities Completed

### 3.1 Beneficiary Identification (January 2024)
- Partnered with 3 local churches in Bamenda
- Identified and registered 50 IDP families meeting vulnerability criteria
- Created beneficiary database with household composition and needs assessment

### 3.2 Food Distributions

| Distribution | Date | Families Reached | Packages Delivered |
|-------------|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1st | January 2024 | 50 | 50 |
| 2nd | February 2024 | 50 | 50 |
| 3rd | March 2024 | 50 | 50 |
| **Total** | | **50** | **150** |

Each food package contained:
- 5kg rice
- 2L cooking oil
- Beans
- Salt
- Seasoning

### 3.3 Coordination
- Regular communication with church partners for logistics planning
- Church premises used as distribution points
- Church leaders assisted with beneficiary verification at each distribution

| Indicator | Target | Achieved |

### Key Achievements
- **100% target reached** — all 50 families received all 3 monthly packages
- **Under budget** — actual spend of 1,450,000 CFA vs. 1,500,000 CFA budget (savings from food donations by church members)
- **Strong church partnerships** — local churches actively participated in identification, verification, and distribution
- **Sustained support** — 3 months of consistent food security for vulnerable families

## 5. Challenges

| Challenge | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Security concerns during transport | Coordinated delivery timing with local contacts |
| Some families relocated during project | Tracked through church networks, maintained contact |
| Rising food prices | Bulk purchasing helped mitigate cost increases |

## 6. Lessons Learned

1. **Church networks are essential** — Local churches provide trusted access to displaced communities and serve as effective distribution infrastructure
2. **Monthly packages provide stability** — Regular, predictable support helps families plan and reduces anxiety
3. **Community contributions matter** — Church members voluntarily donated additional food items, demonstrating community solidarity
4. **Documentation builds trust** — Proper sign-off sheets and photography support accountability and future fundraising

## 7. Sustainability and Recommendations

- Continue partnership with church networks for future interventions
- Explore livelihood support as a follow-up to food relief
- Consider school feeding component for displaced children
- Document model for replication in other affected communities

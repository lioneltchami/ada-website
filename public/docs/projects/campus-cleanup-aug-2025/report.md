# Project Completion Report: Campus Environmental Cleanup

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Reference** | ADA/2025/005 |
| **Project Title** | Campus Environmental Cleanup |
| **Location** | Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon (Student Campus) |
| **Date of Activity** | August 4, 2025 |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Prepared by** | Apoti Development Association (ADA) |

## 1. Executive Summary

On August 4, 2025, the Apoti Development Association (ADA) conducted a single-day environmental cleanup at a student campus in Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon. A team of 8 ADA volunteers, wearing ADA uniforms and red rubber gloves, cleaned the campus grounds, collected waste, and promoted environmental responsibility among the student population. This activity extended ADA's proven cleanup model from city streets to educational institutions.

## 2. Objectives and Achievement

| Objective | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Clean campus grounds and collect waste | ✅ Achieved |
| Deploy ADA-branded trash bins | ✅ Achieved |
| Raise environmental awareness among students | ✅ Achieved |
| Extend cleanup model to educational institutions | ✅ Achieved |

## 3. Activities Conducted

### Preparation
- Coordinated with campus administration for access and logistics
- Secured equipment (partially reused from May 2025 cleanup)
- Organized volunteer team and transport

### Implementation (August 4, 2025)
- 8 ADA volunteers deployed in ADA uniforms and red rubber gloves
- Campus grounds systematically cleaned
- Waste collected and properly disposed via hired truck
- ADA-branded black trash bins placed on campus, labeled: *"APOTI DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION — COMMUNITY SERVICE: CARING FOR OUR ENVIRONMENT"*
- Students engaged on environmental responsibility and civic duty
- Activities documented (photos/video)

## 4. Beneficiaries

| Category | Number |
|----------|--------|
| Direct participants (ADA volunteers) | 8 |
| Indirect beneficiaries (students) | ~500 |

## 5. Outcomes and Impact

- **Campus cleaned:** Grounds cleared of waste and debris
- **Sustained impact:** ADA-branded bins left on campus for ongoing use
- **Awareness raised:** Students exposed to environmental responsibility messaging
- **Model extended:** Successfully demonstrated that ADA's cleanup approach works in educational settings, not just city streets
- **Volunteer visibility:** Team in protective gear and branded uniforms reinforced ADA's professional community service image

## 6. Budget Performance

| Item | Budgeted (CFA) | Actual (CFA) |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| Total | 180,000 | 170,000 |
| **USD equivalent** | **$300** | **$283** |

The project came in 10,000 CFA ($17) under budget, primarily due to equipment reuse from the May 2025 cleanup and efficient logistics coordination.

## 7. Lessons Learned

- Reusing equipment from previous cleanups significantly reduces costs
- Campus administration cooperation is essential for smooth execution
- Student populations are receptive to environmental messaging when paired with visible action
- Branded bins create lasting presence beyond the single-day activity

## 8. Recommendations

- Continue extending cleanup activities to other educational institutions in Buea
- Develop student volunteer partnerships for future campus cleanups
- Track bin usage and condition for maintenance planning
- Consider establishing a campus environmental ambassador program

## 9. Conclusion

The Campus Environmental Cleanup (ADA/2025/005) was successfully completed on August 4, 2025. All objectives were met within budget. This project marks ADA's successful expansion of its environmental cleanup model from city streets to educational institutions, reaching approximately 500 students with civic duty and environmental responsibility messaging.

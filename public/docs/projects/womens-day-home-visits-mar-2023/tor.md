## 1. Background & Context

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) has been serving vulnerable communities in Cameroon's South-West Region since 2021, with a particular focus on widows, orphans, and young women. Through successive projects, ADA has developed a distinctive model of door-to-door, faith-based outreach that prioritizes the dignity and emotional well-being of beneficiaries alongside material support.

International Women's Day, observed annually on March 8, provides a meaningful occasion to honour and uplift women who face daily hardship. In Buea, many widows live in modest wooden plank homes in conditions of isolation and material deprivation. These women — often elderly, without regular income, and disconnected from family support networks — are among the most invisible members of the community. The ongoing socio-economic challenges in the South-West Region, compounded by the effects of the Anglophone crisis, have further eroded the informal safety nets that once sustained vulnerable women.

ADA's Women's Day Home Visits project is designed as an intimate, personal intervention: rather than gathering beneficiaries at a central location, the team will go directly to each widow's home. This approach preserves dignity, accommodates mobility limitations, and communicates a powerful message — that each woman is valued enough to be sought out individually. The project combines material support (food packages) with emotional and spiritual care, delivered on a day dedicated to celebrating women worldwide.

## 2. Project Objectives

### General Objective

To honour and support vulnerable widows in Buea on International Women's Day 2023 through personalized home visits delivering food packages, emotional support, and spiritual encouragement.

### Specific Objectives

1. **SO1:** To deliver comprehensive food packages (rice, cooking oil, soap, seasonings, and bread) to 12 identified widows at their homes on March 8, 2023.
2. **SO2:** To provide personal interaction, prayer, and emotional support to each beneficiary during individual home visits.
3. **SO3:** To preserve the dignity of beneficiaries through an intimate, door-to-door delivery approach rather than a public gathering.
4. **SO4:** To mark International Women's Day with a visible act of community solidarity, with volunteers wearing special Women's Day branded shirts.

### 4.6 Documentation

- Compile project completion report

## 8. Implementation Approach

### Methodology

The project employs a **door-to-door, dignity-centred, faith-based** approach:

1. **Individual Home Visits:** All deliveries are made directly at each widow's home. This intimate approach — rather than gathering beneficiaries at a central point — preserves dignity, accommodates those with mobility challenges, and communicates personal value to each recipient.

2. **Comprehensive Food Packages:** Each package is designed to provide meaningful, practical support: staple food (rice), cooking essentials (oil, seasonings), household items (soap in reusable bucket), and fresh bread as a special Women's Day treat.

3. **Faith-Based Framework:** As a faith-based organization, ADA integrates prayer and spiritual encouragement into each visit. This is offered respectfully and in accordance with each beneficiary's wishes.

4. **Celebratory Identity:** Volunteers wear special Women's Day edition ADA branded t-shirts, creating a visible, celebratory team identity that marks the occasion as one of honour rather than charity.

5. **Volunteer-Driven:** The project is implemented by a team of 6 dedicated volunteers, ensuring personal interaction and community connection at each home.

- All 12 identified widows receive complete food packages at their homes

- All visits are completed within the single day (March 8)
- Volunteer team maintains dignified, respectful approach throughout
- Project completion report is produced within one week

## 12. Risks & Assumptions

### Assumptions

- All 12 identified widows will be at home and available to receive visits on March 8
- Road and transport conditions in Buea will permit access to all beneficiary locations
- Market prices for food items will remain stable during the procurement period
- Vehicle will be available for full-day hire on March 8
- All 6 volunteers will be available for the full day
- Neiforbi Bakery will fulfil the bread order on time

### Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Beneficiary not at home during visit | Medium | Low | Pre-notify beneficiaries of approximate visit time; allow for return visits same day |
| Vehicle breakdown during deliveries | Low | High | Confirm vehicle condition day before; identify backup transport option |
| Rain or adverse weather delays route | Medium | Medium | Plan flexible route; prioritize most distant locations first; carry protective covers for food items |
| Bakery unable to fulfil bread order | Low | Low | Place order 3 days in advance; identify alternative bakery as backup |
| Security concerns restrict movement | Low | High | Monitor situation in advance; postpone if unsafe; adjust route to avoid affected areas |
| Volunteer unavailability on the day | Low | Medium | Brief 2 additional standby volunteers; minimum viable team is 4 |

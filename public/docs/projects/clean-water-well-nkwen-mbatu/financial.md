## 2. Variance Explanation

### Line 2: Borehole Drilling (-50,000 CFA)

The drilling contractor's quote was based on a target depth of 45 meters at a rate of approximately 33,333 CFA per meter. Water was struck at 42 meters depth — 3 meters shallower than estimated — due to favorable geological conditions (fractured zone encountered earlier than predicted by the survey). The contractor invoiced for actual depth drilled (42 m), resulting in a saving of 50,000 CFA.

### Line 9: Contingency (0 CFA variance, fully utilized)

The contingency fund of 40,000 CFA was allocated as follows:
- Additional community meetings (unplanned 4th meeting due to high interest): 15,000 CFA
- Road repair materials for drilling rig access after heavy rains: 15,000 CFA
- Additional protective packaging for solar panel transport: 10,000 CFA

### Savings Allocation

The 50,000 CFA savings from reduced drilling depth was transferred to the village water committee's maintenance fund as a startup contribution, per agreement between ADA and the committee.

## 8. Financial Conclusion

The project was delivered **50,000 CFA (1.9%) under budget**. All budget lines were managed within approved limits. The single variance (drilling depth) resulted in a saving that was productively allocated to the community's maintenance fund, strengthening long-term sustainability.

At approximately $14.72 per beneficiary for a system with 15–20 year lifespan and zero recurring energy costs, this project represents exceptional value for money in the WASH sector.

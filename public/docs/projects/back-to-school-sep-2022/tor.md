## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) has been supporting vulnerable communities in Cameroon since 2021. In 2021, ADA conducted an informal back-to-school drive. For 2022, the process was formalized with proper beneficiary identification through the school headmaster, establishing a structured and repeatable annual program.

## 2. Objective

To provide complete school kits to 20 vulnerable children in an under-resourced community school in Buea, enabling them to attend school with dignity and the materials necessary for learning.

## 3. Scope of Work

### 3.1 Beneficiary Identification
- Collaborate with school headmaster to identify 20 most vulnerable children
- Verify eligibility based on household vulnerability criteria

### 3.2 Procurement
- 20 school uniforms (shirt, shorts/skirt, shoes)
- 20 sets of exercise books (10 books per child)
- 20 sets of school supplies (pens, pencils, ruler, eraser, sharpener)
- 20 school bags

### 3.3 Distribution
- Organize a distribution ceremony at the school
- Distribute kits to identified beneficiaries
- Document the event with photos and attendance records

## 4. Expected Outcomes

- 20 children equipped with complete school kits
- School partnership formalized for future collaboration
- Annual back-to-school tradition established
- Community awareness of ADA's mission increased

## 5. Team Composition

8 volunteers responsible for:
- Beneficiary identification and verification
- Procurement and quality control
- Logistics and transport
- Event coordination and documentation

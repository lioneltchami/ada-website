# Terms of Reference: IDP Girls Baking Training & Startup Kits

**Reference:** ADA/2022/005
**Date:** July 1–31, 2022
**Location:** Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon
**Implementing Organization:** Apoti Development Association (ADA)

---

## 1. Background and Context

The Anglophone crisis in Cameroon's North-West and South-West regions has displaced hundreds of thousands of people since 2016. Among the most vulnerable are young girls and women who fled their communities with nothing, arriving in host towns like Buea without education, skills, or income-generating capacity.

ADA has been providing relief support (food, hygiene kits, spiritual care) to internally displaced persons since 2021. However, the organization recognized that sustainable impact requires moving beyond handouts toward empowerment. This project represents ADA's first vocational training initiative — a deliberate shift from relief to skills-based programming that enables beneficiaries to generate their own income.

## 2. Objective

To equip 15 internally displaced young girls in Buea with practical baking and pastry-making skills, basic business management knowledge, and startup materials to launch small food businesses and achieve economic self-sufficiency.

### Specific Objectives

1. Provide 4 weeks of hands-on baking and pastry training
2. Teach basic business management, pricing, and customer service skills
3. Distribute startup kits containing essential equipment and ingredients
4. Support participants in launching micro-enterprises after graduation

## 3. Target Beneficiaries

- **Number:** 15 young internally displaced girls
- **Profile:** Girls aged 16–25 who fled conflict in NW/SW regions, currently residing in Buea with no vocational skills or stable income
- **Selection Criteria:**
  - Confirmed IDP status (displaced from NW/SW regions)
  - Currently residing in Buea
  - No existing vocational training or stable employment
  - Demonstrated interest and commitment to completing the program
  - Priority given to those with no family support system

## 4. Training Curriculum

### Week 1: Foundations of Baking

| Day | Topic | Practical |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Mon | Introduction to baking: tools, hygiene, safety | Kitchen orientation, equipment handling |
| Tue | Understanding ingredients: flour types, leavening agents, fats | Ingredient identification and measurement |
| Wed | Basic bread baking: mixing, kneading, proofing | White bread loaves |
| Thu | Bread variations: milk bread, coconut bread | Specialty bread production |
| Fri | Review and practice | Independent bread production |

### Week 2: Pastry and Confections

| Day | Topic | Practical |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Mon | Introduction to pastry: dough types, techniques | Puff pastry basics |
| Tue | Meat pies and sausage rolls | Full production cycle |
| Wed | Doughnuts and beignets | Frying techniques, sugar coating |
| Thu | Cakes: pound cake, banana cake | Mixing methods, oven management |
| Fri | Chin-chin, puff-puff, and local snacks | Batch production |

### Week 3: Advanced Techniques and Production

| Day | Topic | Practical |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Mon | Scaling recipes for commercial production | Large-batch bread |
| Tue | Decoration and presentation | Cake decoration basics |
| Wed | Quality control and consistency | Timed production runs |
| Thu | Food preservation and storage | Packaging techniques |
| Fri | Full production simulation | Market-ready products |

### Week 4: Business Skills and Graduation

| Day | Topic | Practical |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Mon | Costing and pricing: calculating profit margins | Price list development |
| Tue | Customer service and marketing | Role-play exercises |
| Wed | Record-keeping and basic accounting | Ledger practice |
| Thu | Business planning: location, target market, growth | Individual business plans |
| Fri | **Graduation ceremony and startup kit distribution** | — |

## 6. Deliverables

1. **Training completion** — 15 girls complete 4-week baking/pastry curriculum
2. **Startup kits distributed** — Each graduate receives a kit containing:
   - 5-litre Mayor cooking oil
   - Plastic bucket (for mixing/storage)
   - Baking supplies (trays, measuring cups, mixing bowls)
   - Initial ingredients (flour, sugar, eggs, butter, yeast)
3. **Business plans** — Each participant develops a basic business plan
4. **Graduation ceremony** — Public event recognizing achievement
5. **Project report** — Documentation of activities, outcomes, and lessons learned

## 9. Expected Outcomes

- 15 IDP girls acquire marketable baking and pastry skills
- Participants gain business management knowledge for self-employment
- Each graduate has materials to immediately begin income generation
- ADA establishes a replicable vocational training model
- Shift from dependency-based relief to sustainable empowerment

## 10. Monitoring and Evaluation

- Daily attendance tracking
- Weekly skills assessments (practical tests)
- Pre/post knowledge surveys
- 3-month follow-up on business activity
- Participant feedback collection

## 11. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Participant dropout | Strong selection process, transport support, volunteer mentoring |
| Ingredient price fluctuation | Bulk purchasing at project start, contingency budget |
| Venue unavailability | Backup venue identified in advance |
| Low product quality | Experienced instructor, progressive skill building |

# Financial Management Policy

**Apoti Development Association (ADA)**  
Registration: N° 415/G.37/D14/VolI/SAAJP  
Effective date: January 2024 · Review: Annual

## Purpose

This policy defines how ADA receives, records, spends, and reports funds so that donors and partners can trust our stewardship.

## Principles

1. **Program priority** — Target 80% of expenditure on direct program delivery.
2. **Low overhead** — Administrative costs capped at 5%; leadership roles are voluntary.
3. **Dual control** — Significant payments require approval by two authorized officers.
4. **Documentation** — Every transaction supported by receipts, invoices, or signed attestations.
5. **Retention** — Financial records kept for a minimum of five years.

## Budgeting & authorization

- Annual budgets approved by the Board of Directors.
- Project budgets aligned with signed Terms of Reference and donor agreements.
- Expenditures above pre-approved thresholds require Board notification.

## Procurement

- Competitive quotes for purchases above 50,000 CFA where practical.
- Conflict of interest declared and managed for vendors linked to staff or board members.

## Reporting

- Quarterly internal summaries to the Board.
- Annual public report published on the website.
- Project-level financial reports available in the [resource library](/resources).

## Anti-corruption

ADA prohibits bribery, misappropriation, and falsification of records. Whistleblowers may report concerns confidentially to `info@apotidev.org`.

_Approved by the ADA Board of Directors._

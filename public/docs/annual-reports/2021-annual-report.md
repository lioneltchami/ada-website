## Message from the Founder

Dear Friends,

In November 2021, three of us walked through the streets of Buea with bags of rice and a simple conviction: that the widows in our community should not be forgotten.

I had seen them — elderly women living alone, some caring for grandchildren, some unable to leave their beds. Women who had given their lives to their families and communities, now invisible to the world around them. I could not unsee what I had seen, and I could not remain still.

ADA was not born from a grand strategy or a large budget. It was born from obedience. God placed a burden on my heart for the vulnerable — widows, orphans, young women without opportunity — and He asked me to take the first step, however small. That first step was $200 and three volunteers knocking on doors.

What we found during those visits broke us and built us at the same time. We found a bedridden widow living in a makeshift shelter with paper walls, caring for her grandchild with almost nothing. We found dignity in the midst of suffering. We found women who needed not just rice, but presence — someone to pray with them, someone to say "you are not forgotten."

This report documents one month of operations, one project, and three beneficiaries. By the world's standards, it is almost nothing. But we believe that faithfulness in small things is the foundation for greater things. We planted a seed in November 2021, and we trust God to bring the harvest.

Thank you for taking the time to read about our beginning. Every great work starts somewhere. This is where ADA started.

In Love, Respect & Service,

**Lionel Tchami Nfada, MSc**
Founder & President
Apoti Development Association

## Our First Project

### First Widow Home Visits

**Reference:** ADA/2021/001
**Date:** November 2021
**Location:** Buea, South-West Region

#### What We Did

In November 2021, a team of three volunteers conducted door-to-door visits to elderly widows in Buea. We carried bags of rice, prayer, and a willingness to listen.

Our objectives were simple:

- Deliver food to widows who might otherwise go without
- Pray with each woman and offer companionship
- Assess living conditions to understand the depth of need in our community
- Document what we found to guide future interventions

#### Who We Helped

We visited three elderly widows, each living in difficult circumstances. One visit remains etched in our memory: we found a bedridden widow living in a makeshift shelter constructed with paper walls. Despite her condition, she was caring for her grandchild. The shelter offered almost no protection from the elements.

This single encounter confirmed everything we believed — that there are vulnerable people in our communities who are suffering in silence, and that even small acts of love can make a difference.

#### What We Learned

1. **The need is real and urgent.** Vulnerable widows in Buea are living in conditions that demand attention.
2. **Presence matters as much as provision.** The women we visited were grateful for the rice, but equally grateful that someone came, sat with them, and prayed.
3. **Small teams can make an impact.** Three volunteers with $200 reached three lives in meaningful ways.
4. **Documentation is essential.** Recording conditions allows us to plan better interventions and advocate for those who cannot advocate for themselves.

#### Outcomes

- 3 widows visited and provided with food supplies
- Living conditions documented for future planning
- ADA officially launched as an organization
- Foundation laid for expanded operations in 2022

## Looking Ahead: 2022 Plans

Our first month showed us what is possible. In 2022, we plan to build on this foundation:

### Expand Project Frequency

- Move from one project to monthly community interventions
- Establish a regular schedule of widow visits and food distribution

### Grow Our Reach

- Extend support beyond widows to include orphans and young women
- Increase the number of beneficiaries served per project
- Explore education and skills training programmes

### Build Our Team

- Recruit and train additional volunteers
- Establish volunteer coordination processes

- Build an online presence to share our work with the world

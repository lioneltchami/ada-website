## Message from the Founder

Dear friends and partners,

2023 was a defining year for ADA — not because we did more, but because we chose to do *fewer things better*.

After an ambitious 2022 with seven projects across two cities, we made a deliberate strategic decision: consolidate our strengths, deepen our impact, and expand only where we could sustain quality. The result was four carefully chosen initiatives that delivered measurable, lasting change in the lives of 242 people across three cities.

This year marked two significant firsts. We launched our first long-term program in Yaoundé — a six-month vocational training for young women in Cameroon's capital. And we brought our back-to-school initiative to Bamenda for the first time, partnering with four local schools to support 20 orphaned children. Both represent ADA's evolution from a reactive organization to one that plans strategically and invests for sustained outcomes.

The numbers tell a story of stewardship: we delivered all four projects 3.4% under budget while maintaining — and in many cases exceeding — our impact targets. Eight of ten women in our skills training are now earning income. Two have opened their own businesses. Every sponsored child in Bamenda remained in school through the end of the year.

ADA is no longer a startup finding its footing. We are an established organization making deliberate choices about where to invest our limited resources for maximum kingdom impact. We are learning that depth matters more than breadth, and that sustainable growth requires the discipline to say "not yet" to good opportunities so we can say "yes" to the right ones.

Thank you for walking this journey with us. Your prayers, partnership, and generosity make this work possible.

In service,

**Lionel Tchami Nfada, MSc**
Founder & President
Apoti Development Association

## Year in Review — Project Summaries

### 1. Women's Day Home Visits

**March 8, 2023 | Buea | Budget: $600 | Actual: $575 (345,000 CFA)**

On International Women's Day, ADA volunteers conducted door-to-door visits to 12 widows in Buea. Rather than hosting a large public event, we chose an intimate, dignity-centred approach — meeting each woman in her own home with gifts, prayer, and genuine conversation.

Each visit included provision of essential household items, personal care packages, and extended time for fellowship. This model reflects ADA's belief that true empowerment begins with being seen and valued as an individual, not as a number in a crowd. The home visit format allowed our team to assess each widow's specific needs and connect them with ongoing community support networks.

---

### 2. Young Women Skills Training

**April – October 2023 | Yaoundé | Budget: $2,000 | Actual: $1,917 (1,150,000 CFA)**

ADA's flagship program of 2023 — and our first initiative in Cameroon's capital city. Ten young women from vulnerable backgrounds enrolled in a six-month vocational training program covering hairdressing, tailoring, and small business management.

This was ADA's second vocational training cohort and our most successful to date. By program completion, 8 of 10 participants were earning independent income, and 2 had opened their own small businesses. The program included not only technical skills but also financial literacy, customer service, and business planning — equipping graduates for long-term economic independence rather than short-term employment. Launching in Yaoundé demonstrated ADA's capacity to replicate proven models in new geographic contexts.

---

### 3. City Cleanup — Labour Day

**May 1, 2023 | Buea | Budget: $400 | Actual: $383 (230,000 CFA)**

For the third consecutive year, ADA organized a community cleanup on Labour Day, mobilizing volunteers to clean public spaces, clear drainage channels, and promote environmental stewardship in Buea. An estimated 200 community members benefited indirectly from improved sanitation and public spaces.

This annual tradition has become a signature ADA event — a visible demonstration that faith-based organizations contribute to civic life beyond spiritual programs. The cleanup also serves as a community engagement and volunteer recruitment opportunity, introducing new people to ADA's broader mission.

---

### 4. Back-to-School Drive 2023

**August – December 2023 | Bamenda | Budget: $1,600 | Actual: $1,567 (940,000 CFA)**

ADA's first back-to-school initiative in Bamenda supported 20 orphaned children with school fees, uniforms, books, and supplies across 4 partner schools in the North-West Region. This expansion from Buea to Bamenda was strategic — the anglophone crisis has left many children in the North-West without parental support or educational access.

The program achieved a 100% school retention rate through December, with every sponsored child remaining enrolled and attending classes. ADA's partnership model — working directly with school administrators for accountability — proved effective in a new city context. Monthly check-ins ensured children were attending, performing, and receiving adequate support.

## Challenges & Lessons Learned

### Challenges

**Multi-city logistics**
Operating across three cities in three regions introduced coordination complexity. Travel between Buea, Bamenda, and Yaoundé requires significant time and cost, and communication across distances demands more structured reporting systems.

**Anglophone crisis impact**
The ongoing socio-political situation in the North-West and South-West regions continues to affect program delivery, particularly in Bamenda. School closures, movement restrictions, and community displacement remain real operational risks.

**Scaling with limited staff**
With a lean team, expanding to a third city stretched our human capacity. Each new location requires local relationships, oversight, and accountability structures that take time to build.

### Lessons Learned

**Depth over breadth delivers better outcomes**
Reducing from 7 projects (2022) to 4 projects (2023) allowed us to invest more time, resources, and attention in each initiative. The result: higher completion rates, better follow-up, and more measurable impact.

**Long-term programs create lasting change**
The 6-month skills training in Yaoundé produced dramatically better outcomes than shorter interventions. Sustained engagement allows for skill mastery, relationship building, and genuine transformation.

**Local partnerships are essential for expansion**
Our success in Bamenda (4 school partnerships, 100% retention) demonstrates that working through established local institutions is more effective than building parallel structures.

**Strategic restraint is a strength**
Saying "no" to good opportunities in order to focus on the right ones is not a limitation — it's a sign of organizational maturity.

## 1. Executive Summary

On December 18, 2024, the Apoti Development Association (ADA) conducted its third annual Christmas Giving campaign at a children's home in Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon. A team of 8 ADA volunteers delivered a full Christmas celebration program to 50 orphaned children, distributing food supplies, toys, and treats. This was ADA's largest Christmas campaign to date, growing from 30 beneficiaries in 2022 to 50 in 2024, and evolving from a simple supply drop-off to a comprehensive celebration event.

## 2. Activities Delivered

### 2.1 Celebration Program
- **Singing and clapping** — Volunteers and children participated in Christmas songs together
- **Worship session** — Faith-based worship component as part of ADA's mission
- **Interactive activities** — Engaging games and activities with the children

### 2.2 Distributions
- **Food supplies:** Mayor brand cooking oil (10L x 3), rice (5kg bags x 10), soap bars (bulk), toilet paper (bulk), juice/drinks (crates)
- **Toys and gifts:** Individual gifts for all 50 children
- **Treats and snacks:** Pastries and sweets for all children

### 2.3 Volunteer Presence
- 8 ADA volunteers present in branded white polo shirts ("Love, Respect & Service") and Santa hats
- Full ADA branded presence maintained throughout the event

## 3. Observations

- The orphanage had prepared a hand-painted welcome sign: *"OUR DEAR VISITORS WE THANK YOU FOR GIFT AND SUPPORT"*
- Children were seated in small colorful chairs in a room with educational posters (numbers chart) on the walls
- The environment was modest but well-maintained, indicating an active learning space
- Children were engaged and responsive throughout the program
- The event moved beyond material giving to genuine emotional connection

## 6. Challenges

- None significant. The event was delivered as planned within budget.

## 7. Lessons Learned

1. A full celebration program creates deeper impact than a simple supply drop-off
2. Branded volunteer presence reinforces ADA's identity and professionalism
3. Scaling from 30 to 50 children was manageable with 8 volunteers
4. Advance coordination with the orphanage ensured smooth logistics

## 8. Recommendations

1. Continue annual Christmas campaign with target of 60+ children for 2025
2. Maintain the full celebration program format (not just drop-off)
3. Consider adding an educational component (books, school supplies) in future editions
4. Document beneficiary stories for donor communications

## 5. Variance Analysis

Total project expenditure was 35,000 CFA ($58.33 USD) under budget, representing a 29.2% savings. The following factors contributed to the positive variance:

### 5.1 Rice Procurement (−6,000 CFA)

Budgeted at 15,000 CFA per 25kg bag based on retail pricing. Actual purchase was made at Buea Main Market at a wholesale rate of 13,000 CFA per bag due to bulk purchase of 3 bags from a single vendor.

### 5.2 Transport Savings (−5,000 CFA)

One founding member volunteered personal vehicle and fuel for one of the three home visit trips, eliminating one transport cost of 5,000 CFA. The remaining two trips were conducted via hired motorcycle transport at the budgeted rate.

### 5.3 Communication Savings (−1,000 CFA)

Initial budget assumed voice calls for coordination. In practice, WhatsApp messaging (using existing data bundles) reduced the need for airtime purchases. Only 4,000 CFA of airtime was required for calls to beneficiaries without smartphones.

### 5.4 Contingency Underspend (−23,000 CFA)

The contingency line was conservatively budgeted at 40,000 CFA to cover unforeseen costs for this first-ever project. Actual miscellaneous expenses (water sachets for visits, small household gifts) totaled only 17,000 CFA. The remaining contingency was not needed.

### 5.5 Conclusion

The under-spend reflects prudent financial management and community goodwill (volunteer transport), not a failure to deliver planned activities. All three widow households received the full planned support package.

## 6. Financial Controls

### 6.1 Fund Management Procedures

| Control | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Custodian** | Single designated Treasurer responsible for all cash holdings |
| **Storage** | Funds kept in locked personal safe until disbursement |
| **Disbursement** | Cash disbursed only against approved budget lines |
| **Dual Authorization** | All expenditures above 10,000 CFA required verbal approval from Project Lead |
| **Receipt Collection** | Physical receipts obtained for all purchases |
| **Record Keeping** | All transactions recorded in project cash book on date of expenditure |

### 6.2 Limitations Acknowledged

As ADA's inaugural project with a small budget, the following limitations are noted:

- All transactions were cash-based (no bank account established at this stage)
- Some market purchases (e.g., water sachets) did not have formal receipts; handwritten notes were recorded instead
- No external audit was conducted due to the small project size

### 6.3 Improvements for Future Projects

- Open dedicated ADA bank account for fund management
- Implement dual-signatory requirements for all disbursements
- Photograph all receipts for digital backup
- Engage independent reviewer for projects exceeding 500,000 CFA

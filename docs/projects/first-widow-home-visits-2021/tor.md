## 1. Background & Context

Widows in Cameroon, particularly in the South-West Region, face severe socio-economic marginalization. Cultural practices often strip widows of inheritance rights, leaving elderly women without land, shelter, or financial resources after the death of a spouse. In rural communities surrounding Buea, widows who have outlived their extended family networks are among the most invisible and vulnerable members of society — often surviving on the charity of neighbours or subsisting on less than one meal per day.

The ongoing Anglophone crisis, which escalated from 2016 onward, has compounded these vulnerabilities. Displacement, destruction of livelihoods, and the breakdown of community support structures have left many elderly widows completely isolated. Young family members who might have provided care have fled to francophone regions or abroad. Health facilities and social services in the South-West Region operate at reduced capacity, and government social safety nets remain virtually non-existent for this demographic. Elderly widows living alone face food insecurity, untreated illness, and profound emotional distress with no institutional recourse.

It is within this context that the Apoti Development Association (ADA) was founded in 2021 with a mission to serve the most vulnerable. This inaugural project — the First Widow Home Visits — represents ADA's founding intervention: a direct, community-based response to identify and support elderly widows living in extreme poverty in the Buea municipality. The project addresses immediate food needs while establishing a baseline understanding of the living conditions and support requirements of this population.

## 2. Project Objectives

### General Objective

To provide immediate humanitarian relief and psychosocial support to elderly widows living alone in extreme poverty in Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon.

### Specific Objectives

1. **SO1:** To deliver food assistance (rice packages) to at least 3 identified elderly widows within their homes during November 2021.
2. **SO2:** To conduct needs assessments of the living conditions, health status, and support networks of each beneficiary widow.
3. **SO3:** To provide emotional and spiritual support through prayer, companionship, and active listening during home visits.
4. **SO4:** To document the conditions of vulnerable widows in the community as a foundation for future programme design and advocacy.

### Selection Criteria

Beneficiaries shall be identified through community referral and must meet the following criteria:

- Widowed (husband deceased)
- Living alone or as sole caregiver for minor dependents
- No regular income or family financial support
- Residing in substandard or precarious housing
- Experiencing food insecurity
- Identified by community members, church leaders, or neighbours as being in urgent need

### Indirect Beneficiaries

- Dependents living with the widows (e.g., grandchildren)
- Surrounding community members who benefit from awareness raised about widow vulnerability

## 4. Scope of Work / Activities

The project shall undertake the following activities:

### 4.1 Beneficiary Identification & Mapping
- Engage community leaders, church pastors, and neighbours to identify eligible widows
- Verify eligibility through brief community consultations
- Map locations of identified beneficiaries for visit planning

### 4.2 Procurement of Relief Items
- Purchase 25kg bags of rice (one per beneficiary household)
- Procure supplementary food items as budget allows (cooking oil, salt)
- Prepare food packages for distribution

### 4.3 Home Visits & Food Delivery
- Conduct door-to-door visits to each identified widow
- Deliver food packages directly to beneficiary homes
- Ensure dignified and respectful delivery process

### 4.4 Needs Assessment
- Observe and document housing conditions (structure, roofing, sanitation)
- Assess health status and mobility of each beneficiary
- Identify immediate and longer-term needs (medical, shelter, food security)
- Record number and status of dependents

### 4.5 Psychosocial & Spiritual Support
- Provide prayer and spiritual encouragement
- Offer companionship and active listening
- Assess emotional well-being and signs of isolation or distress

### 4.6 Documentation & Reporting
- Photograph living conditions (with consent)
- Complete beneficiary assessment forms
- Compile visit reports and project completion report

## 8. Implementation Approach

### Methodology

The project employs a **door-to-door, faith-based, community-referral** approach:

1. **Community Referral:** Beneficiaries are identified through trusted community networks — church leaders, neighbours, and local women's groups — rather than through formal registration systems that may exclude the most isolated individuals.

2. **Door-to-Door Delivery:** All services are delivered directly at the beneficiary's home. This approach recognizes that elderly widows with mobility challenges or caregiving responsibilities cannot travel to distribution points.

3. **Faith-Based Framework:** As a faith-based organization, ADA integrates prayer and spiritual support into its service delivery. This is offered respectfully and in accordance with each beneficiary's wishes.

4. **Dignity-Centred:** Visits are conducted with respect for the autonomy and dignity of each beneficiary. Assistance is offered, not imposed. Consent is obtained for all documentation.

5. **Volunteer-Driven:** The project is implemented by a team of 3 dedicated volunteers, ensuring low overhead and direct community engagement.

The project will be considered successful if:

- Needs assessment forms are completed for each beneficiary

- At least 2 actionable recommendations for future programming are identified

## 12. Risks & Assumptions

### Assumptions

- Community leaders and church networks will cooperate in identifying eligible beneficiaries
- Identified widows will consent to receive visits and assistance
- Road and transport conditions in Buea will permit access to beneficiary locations during November
- Market prices for food items will remain stable during the procurement period
- Volunteers will remain available for the full project duration

### Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Security deterioration due to Anglophone crisis restricts movement | Medium | High | Monitor security situation weekly; postpone visits if unsafe; identify alternative access routes |
| Identified beneficiaries refuse assistance or are unavailable | Low | Medium | Maintain a reserve list of eligible widows; reschedule visits as needed |
| Market price increases exceed budget | Low | Medium | Procure early in project cycle; adjust quantities within budget envelope |
| Volunteer attrition | Low | Medium | Brief additional community members as standby volunteers |
| Adverse weather limits access to rural locations | Medium | Low | Schedule visits on dry days; allow flexibility in weekly planning |

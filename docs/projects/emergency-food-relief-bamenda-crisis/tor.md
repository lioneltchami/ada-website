# Terms of Reference: Emergency Food Relief - Bamenda Crisis

**Reference:** ADA/2024/001
**Organization:** Apoti Development Association (ADA)
**Date:** January 15 – April 15, 2024
**Duration:** 3 months
**Location:** Bamenda, North-West Region, Cameroon

---

## 1. Background and Context

The ongoing Anglophone crisis in Cameroon's North-West and South-West regions continues to displace families from rural areas into urban centers, particularly Bamenda. Displaced families arrive with nothing — no income, no shelter stability, and children out of school. Local churches have become the first point of contact for these internally displaced persons (IDPs), providing initial shelter and community support but lacking resources for sustained food assistance.

## 2. Project Objective

To provide emergency food relief to 50 internally displaced families (150+ individuals) in Bamenda over a 3-month period through monthly food package distributions coordinated with local church partners.

## 3. Scope of Work

### 3.1 Beneficiary Identification
- Partner with 3 local churches to identify and verify 50 IDP families in urgent need
- Prioritize families with children, elderly, and those with no income source
- Maintain beneficiary register with household details

### 3.2 Food Package Procurement and Distribution
- Procure monthly food packages for 50 families over 3 months (150 total packages)
- Each package contains: 5kg rice, 2L cooking oil, beans, salt, seasoning
- Conduct 3 monthly distribution events at church-based distribution points

### 3.3 Coordination and Logistics
- Coordinate with local church partners for distribution point logistics
- Arrange transport and delivery for each monthly distribution
- Ensure proper storage and handling of food items

### 3.4 Documentation and Reporting
- Document all distributions with beneficiary sign-off
- Photograph distribution events
- Produce final project report and financial statement

## 1. Executive Summary

This project was ADA's most comprehensive single-day outreach to date, combining material aid with financial assistance and emotional support for 15 widows in Buea. The initiative successfully established ADA's multi-pronged intervention model, delivering food supplies, household essentials, cash assistance, and companionship through both a community gathering and individual street visits. All 15 targeted beneficiaries were reached, and the project was completed under budget at 460,000 CFA ($767 USD).

## 2. Activities Implemented

### 2.1 Preparation Phase
- Identification and verification of 15 widow beneficiaries in Buea.
- Procurement of rice, cooking oil, handbags, plastic buckets, and supplies.
- Preparation of cash envelopes (15 × 3,000 CFA).
- Venue arrangement and logistics coordination.
- Volunteer briefing and role assignment (8 volunteers).

### 2.2 Community Gathering
- Central gathering organized for beneficiaries in Buea.
- Opening prayer and fellowship session.
- Distribution of complete aid packages to attending widows.
- Group encouragement and community bonding.

### 2.3 Individual Street Visits
- Door-to-door delivery for widows unable to attend the central gathering.
- Personal companionship and one-on-one encouragement.
- Direct handover of aid packages at beneficiaries' homes.

### Key Achievements
- **100% beneficiary reach** — All 15 identified widows received comprehensive support.
- **Multi-pronged model established** — Successfully combined material, financial, and emotional support in a single intervention.
- **Under-budget delivery** — Project completed at 460,000 CFA vs. 480,000 CFA budgeted (savings of 20,000 CFA).
- **Community strengthening** — Gathering with prayer fostered social bonds among beneficiaries.
- **Dignified delivery** — Combination of group and individual visits ensured all widows were reached respectfully.

## 4. Challenges & Lessons Learned

### Challenges
- Coordinating logistics for both a central gathering and individual home visits on the same day.
- Ensuring all beneficiaries were informed and available.
- Transporting bulk supplies (25kg rice bags) to individual homes.

### Lessons Learned
- The multi-pronged model (food + essentials + cash + companionship) is highly effective and should be replicated.
- Cash envelopes, even at modest amounts, provide beneficiaries with agency to address personal priorities.
- Community gatherings add significant emotional value beyond material distribution.
- A team of 8 volunteers is adequate for serving 15 beneficiaries across both delivery modes.

## 6. Recommendations

1. **Scale the model** — Replicate this multi-pronged approach for future widow support projects with larger beneficiary groups.
2. **Increase cash component** — Consider higher cash envelope amounts as beneficiaries reported this as highly impactful.
3. **Follow-up visits** — Schedule 30-day follow-up to assess sustained impact of food supplies.
4. **Document the model** — Formalize the combined material + financial + emotional support approach as ADA's standard intervention framework.

## 7. Conclusion

The Widow Support — Food & Financial Aid project successfully demonstrated that comprehensive, dignified support can be delivered efficiently within a modest budget. By combining food security, household essentials, direct financial assistance, and emotional companionship, ADA established a replicable model for future community interventions. The project was delivered on time, under budget, and reached 100% of targeted beneficiaries.

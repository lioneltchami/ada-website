## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) is a faith-based organization empowering vulnerable communities in Cameroon since 2021, with programs supporting widows, orphans, and young women. The Young Women Empowerment Conference represents ADA's largest single event, designed to inspire and equip young women and girls through faith-based teaching, mentorship, and personal development.

## 2. Objectives

1. Deliver a two-day faith-based empowerment conference for 80+ young women and girls in Buea.
2. Provide teaching sessions on faith, purpose, and personal development.
3. Facilitate Bible study and spiritual growth opportunities.
4. Establish mentorship connections between experienced women leaders and young participants.
5. Distribute printed program materials and notebooks to support active learning.

## 3. Target Group

- Young women and girls (teenagers to young adults) in Buea and surrounding areas
- Religiously diverse participants from the local community
- Minimum 80 beneficiaries

## 4. Activities

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Teaching sessions | Faith, purpose, and personal development led by 2 female speakers |
| Bible study | Guided study sessions with active note-taking |
| Mentorship connections | Linking participants with women leaders and facilitators |
| Material distribution | Printed programs (100 copies), notebooks, and pens for all participants |
| Refreshments | Meals/snacks provided for all attendees across both days |

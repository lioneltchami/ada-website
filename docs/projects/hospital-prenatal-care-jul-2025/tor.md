## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) is a faith-based organization empowering vulnerable communities in Cameroon since 2021. This project represents ADA's first hospital-based maternal care program, establishing a partnership with the Sub-Divisional Hospital (CMA) Muea-Buea to support new mothers who cannot afford basic supplies for their newborns.

The hospital identified the most vulnerable mothers — those unable to purchase essential items for their first weeks with a newborn — as the target beneficiaries.

## 2. Objectives

1. Provide 25 new mothers with comprehensive maternal care kits containing essential baby supplies
2. Establish a working partnership with CMA Muea-Buea hospital and nursing staff
3. Ensure dignified, personal delivery of kits to mothers in the hospital setting
4. Demonstrate ADA's commitment to maternal and infant welfare

## 3. Scope of Work

### 3.1 Kit Composition

Each branded red bucket (with ADA logo sticker: *"The Joy of the More Vulnerable is our Priority"*) contains:

- Diapers (Softcare/Floom brands)
- Baby clothes (onesie, undershirts, shoes)
- Soap and hygiene items
- Towels and blankets

### 3.2 Activities

- Coordinate with CMA Muea-Buea hospital administration and nursing staff
- Procure and assemble 25 maternal care kits
- Conduct multiple hospital visits over 10 days to distribute kits
- Personally present kits to mothers holding their newborns
- Document activities through photography

### 3.3 Team Composition

- 5 ADA volunteers in branded green-and-white uniforms with green berets
- Hospital nursing staff (coordination and beneficiary identification)

## 4. Expected Outcomes

- 25 new mothers receive complete branded care kits
- Functional partnership established with government hospital staff
- Dignified, personal presentation of kits to each mother
- Photographic documentation of the program
- Foundation laid for future hospital-based maternal care initiatives

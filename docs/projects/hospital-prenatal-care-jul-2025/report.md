## 1. Executive Summary

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) successfully completed its first hospital-based maternal care program at the Sub-Divisional Hospital (CMA) Muea-Buea in the South-West Region of Cameroon. Over a 10-day period (July 26 – August 5, 2025), a team of 5 ADA volunteers conducted multiple visits to the hospital, distributing 25 branded maternal care kits to new mothers identified by hospital nursing staff as the most vulnerable.

## 2. Activities Conducted

### 2.1 Preparation Phase

- Coordinated with CMA Muea-Buea hospital administration to establish partnership
- Procured all kit materials: red buckets, diapers (Softcare and Floom brands), baby clothes, soap, towels, blankets, and hygiene essentials
- Branded red buckets with ADA logo stickers (*"The Joy of the More Vulnerable is our Priority"*)
- Assembled 25 complete maternal care kits
- Prepared volunteer uniforms (green-and-white branded polos with green berets)

### 2.2 Distribution Phase

- Conducted multiple visits to CMA Muea-Buea over 10 days
- Worked alongside hospital nursing staff who identified beneficiary mothers
- Personally presented kits to 25 new mothers holding their newborns
- Volunteers wore ADA-branded uniforms (t-shirts reading: *"APOTI DEVELOPMENT ASSOCIATION — Love, Respect & Service — 1 John 4:8"*)
- Documented all activities through photography

## 3. Results and Outcomes

### 3.1 Key Achievements

1. **Full target reached:** All 25 maternal care kits successfully distributed to new mothers
2. **Hospital partnership:** Established working relationship with CMA Muea-Buea nursing staff for beneficiary identification and coordination
3. **Dignified delivery:** Each kit personally presented to mothers by volunteers working alongside nurses
4. **Brand visibility:** ADA presence established in hospital setting through branded kits, uniforms, and multiple visits
5. **Documentation:** Complete photographic record of activities

### 3.2 Kit Contents Delivered (per mother)

- 1 red bucket branded with ADA logo
- 1 pack of diapers (Softcare/Floom)
- 1 set of baby clothes (onesie, undershirts, shoes)
- Soap and hygiene items
- Towel and blanket

## 4. Challenges and Lessons Learned

### 4.1 Significance

This project represents ADA's first hospital-based maternal care initiative, establishing a model for future programs. The partnership with a government hospital demonstrates ADA's ability to work within institutional frameworks to reach vulnerable populations.

### 4.2 Lessons Learned

- Hospital staff are effective partners for identifying the most vulnerable beneficiaries
- Multiple visits over an extended period allow reaching mothers at different stages
- Branded kits create lasting visibility and association with ADA's mission
- Personal presentation by volunteers creates meaningful human connection

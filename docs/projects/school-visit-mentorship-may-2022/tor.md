## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) has been supporting vulnerable communities in Cameroon since 2021. Previous interventions focused on one-time distributions to beneficiaries. This project represents a strategic shift toward relationship-based engagement, working within an existing school structure rather than organizing standalone events.

## 2. Objective

To pilot an in-school mentorship and engagement model that builds lasting relationships with students and school administration, providing mentorship, encouragement, and nutritional support to secondary school students in Buea.

## 3. Scope of Work

### 3.1 Activities

- **In-school visit** during regular school hours
- **Mentorship session** — encouragement, life skills, and academic motivation
- **Feeding program** — provision of food and refreshments to 25 students
- **Relationship building** — establish ongoing partnership with the school

### 3.2 Target Beneficiaries

- 25 secondary school students (ages 10–14, predominantly boys)
- Located in Buea, South-West Region

## 4. Expected Outcomes

1. 25 students engaged through in-school mentorship
2. Feeding program successfully delivered
3. Ongoing relationship established with school administration
4. In-school engagement model piloted and documented for replication

## 5. Team Composition

- 6 ADA volunteers responsible for mentorship delivery, logistics, and coordination with school staff

## 1. Executive Summary

The Prenatal Care Support Program has made strong progress since its launch in January 2025. All 25 target beneficiaries have been enrolled, 15 safe deliveries have been completed with zero maternal complications, and partnerships with 2 local clinics are functioning effectively. The program is on track to achieve its objectives within the planned timeline and budget.

## 5. Activities Completed

1. **Beneficiary identification and enrollment** — 25 vulnerable pregnant women identified through community referrals and clinic partners, all enrolled by end of February 2025
2. **Prenatal vitamin distribution** — Monthly supply of vitamins provided to all 25 women since enrollment
3. **Clinic check-ups** — Regular prenatal consultations facilitated at 2 partner clinics
4. **Hospital deliveries** — 15 women have delivered safely at partner clinics with fees covered by ADA
5. **Postnatal care kits** — 15 kits distributed to mothers after delivery
6. **Transport support** — Transport costs covered for beneficiaries attending clinic appointments
7. **Coordination meetings** — Regular meetings with clinic staff to monitor progress and address issues

## 6. Challenges and Mitigation

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Some beneficiaries live far from clinics | Transport costs covered; coordination with nearest partner clinic |
| Rising cost of vitamins | Bulk purchasing negotiated with supplier |
| Follow-up with beneficiaries after delivery | Phone-based check-ins and home visits by coordinators |

## 7. Lessons Learned

1. Early enrollment and relationship-building with beneficiaries improves attendance at check-ups
2. Clinic partnerships are essential for sustainable service delivery and referrals
3. Transport support is a critical enabler — without it, some women would miss appointments
4. Community trust in the program has grown, with new referrals coming in regularly

## 8. Next Steps (Remaining Program Period)

2. Distribute remaining 10 postnatal care kits
3. Continue monthly vitamin supply until all women have delivered
4. Conduct final postnatal follow-up visits for all 25 beneficiaries

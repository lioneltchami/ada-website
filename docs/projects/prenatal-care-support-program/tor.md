# Terms of Reference: Prenatal Care Support Program

**Reference:** ADA/2025/001
**Organization:** Apoti Development Association (ADA)
**Status:** Ongoing
**Start Date:** January 1, 2025
**Location:** Bamenda, North-West Region, Cameroon

---

## 1. Background and Context

Vulnerable pregnant women in Bamenda face significant barriers to accessing prenatal care and safe hospital deliveries. Many cannot afford prenatal vitamins, routine check-ups, or hospital delivery fees, forcing them to deliver at home without medical supervision. This increases the risk of maternal and neonatal complications, preventable deaths, and long-term health consequences.

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) established the Prenatal Care Support Program to address this gap by covering essential healthcare costs for vulnerable pregnant women through partnerships with local clinics.

## 2. Objectives

### Overall Objective
To reduce maternal and neonatal health risks among vulnerable pregnant women in Bamenda by ensuring access to prenatal care, safe hospital deliveries, and postnatal support.

### Specific Objectives
1. Provide prenatal vitamins and regular check-ups for 25 vulnerable pregnant women over 6 months
2. Cover hospital delivery fees to ensure safe, medically supervised births
3. Provide postnatal care kits to support mother and newborn health after delivery
4. Establish sustainable partnerships with local clinics for continued service delivery

## 3. Target Beneficiaries

- **Direct beneficiaries:** 25 pregnant women who cannot afford prenatal care or hospital delivery fees
- **Indirect beneficiaries:** Newborns, families, and the broader community
- **Location:** Bamenda, North-West Region, Cameroon

### Selection Criteria
- Pregnant women unable to afford prenatal care
- Resident in Bamenda area
- Referred by community leaders, churches, or clinic staff
- Priority given to widows, single mothers, and displaced women

## 4. Activities

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Prenatal vitamins | Monthly supply of vitamins for 25 women over 6 months |
| Hospital delivery fees | Coverage of delivery costs at 2 partner clinics |
| Postnatal care kits | Essential supplies for mother and newborn after delivery |
| Transport support | Assistance with transport costs to attend clinic appointments |
| Clinic coordination | Regular coordination meetings with 2 partner clinics |
| Beneficiary follow-up | Monitoring health outcomes and providing ongoing support |

## 5. Implementation Team

| Role | Number | Responsibility |
|------|--------|----------------|
| ADA Coordinators | 2 | Program management, beneficiary enrollment, monitoring |
| Partner Clinic Staff | 2 | Medical care delivery, health monitoring, referrals |

## 7. Expected Outcomes

1. 25 pregnant women receive complete prenatal care including vitamins and check-ups
2. 25 safe hospital deliveries with zero maternal complications
3. 25 postnatal care kits distributed to new mothers
4. Sustainable partnerships established with 2 local clinics
5. Reduced home deliveries among target beneficiaries

## 8. Monitoring and Reporting

- Monthly check-ins with beneficiaries and clinic staff
- Tracking of deliveries, health outcomes, and complications
- Interim progress report at program midpoint
- Final completion report at program end
- Financial reporting against approved budget

## 9. Duration and Timeline

- **Start:** January 1, 2025
- **Duration:** Approximately 12 months (enrollment through final postnatal follow-up)
- **Prenatal phase:** 6 months of vitamin supply and check-ups
- **Delivery phase:** Rolling, as each woman reaches term
- **Postnatal phase:** Follow-up support after delivery

## 10. Partnerships

The program operates in partnership with 2 clinics in Bamenda that provide:
- Prenatal consultations and check-ups
- Safe delivery services
- Postnatal health monitoring
- Referral of eligible beneficiaries

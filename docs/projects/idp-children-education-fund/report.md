# Interim Progress Report: IDP Children Education Fund

**Reference:** ADA/2025/006
**Reporting Period:** September 1, 2025 – May 2026
**Report Date:** May 14, 2026
**Status:** ONGOING
**Location:** Bamenda, North-West Region, Cameroon

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The IDP Children Education Fund has successfully enrolled and maintained 15 internally displaced children in local schools in Bamenda since September 2025. All children remain actively attending school, and psychosocial support sessions have been delivered as planned. The project is on track to complete the full academic year with no dropouts recorded.

## 2. Project Context

Children of families displaced by the Anglophone crisis in Cameroon's North-West Region face permanent educational exclusion due to their families' inability to afford school fees. ADA intervened to cover full school year costs for 15 identified children, ensuring they access quality education despite their displacement.

## 3. Activities Completed

### 3.1 Education Support
- ✅ School fees paid for all 15 children (full academic year)
- ✅ School uniforms procured and distributed
- ✅ Textbooks and school supplies provided to all beneficiaries

### 3.2 Psychosocial Support
- ✅ Group psychosocial support sessions conducted by trained facilitator
- ✅ Ongoing emotional wellbeing monitoring

### 3.3 Coordination
- ✅ School liaison officers assigned and active
- ✅ Regular attendance monitoring in place
- ✅ Communication maintained with families

## 5. Beneficiary Profile

- **Total beneficiaries:** 15 children
- **Gender:** Mixed (boys and girls from IDP families)
- **Origin:** Families displaced from conflict-affected areas of the North-West and South-West regions
- **Current location:** Bamenda and surrounding areas

## 6. Challenges

- Some families remain highly mobile due to ongoing insecurity, requiring flexible coordination
- Psychosocial needs are deeper than initially anticipated for some children; additional follow-up sessions recommended
- Rising costs of school supplies mid-year managed within contingency

## 8. Lessons Learned

- Early enrollment (before school year starts) ensures children are not excluded from class lists
- Combining education support with psychosocial care addresses the whole child, not just access
- School liaison officers are critical for real-time attendance tracking and early intervention

## 9. Next Steps

- Complete the academic year (June/July 2026)
- Conduct end-of-year psychosocial assessment
- Collect final academic results for all 15 children
- Prepare final narrative and financial report
- Assess feasibility of continuing support for the next academic year

## 10. Conclusion

The IDP Children Education Fund is achieving its core objective: ensuring displaced children access education despite their families' circumstances. All 15 children are enrolled, attending, and receiving holistic support. The project demonstrates that targeted, modest investment can prevent permanent educational exclusion for conflict-affected children.

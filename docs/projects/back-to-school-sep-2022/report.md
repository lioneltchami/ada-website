# Project Report: Back-to-School 2022

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Reference** | ADA/2022/006 |
| **Project Title** | Back-to-School Kit Distribution 2022 |
| **Organization** | Apoti Development Association (ADA) |
| **Location** | Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon |
| **Period** | September 1–30, 2022 |
| **Budget** | $1,000 USD (~600,000 CFA) |
| **Actual Spend** | $975 USD (~585,000 CFA) |
| **Beneficiaries** | 20 vulnerable children |
| **Team** | 8 volunteers |

## 1. Executive Summary

ADA successfully completed its second annual Back-to-School drive in September 2022, providing complete school kits to 20 vulnerable children at an under-resourced community school in Buea. The project was delivered under budget at 585,000 CFA ($975) due to savings from bulk uniform purchases. This edition formalized the process established informally in 2021, with proper beneficiary identification through the school headmaster.

## 2. Activities Completed

### 2.1 Beneficiary Identification
- Partnered with school headmaster to identify 20 most vulnerable children
- Formalized selection criteria and verification process
- Established documentation for future reference

### 2.2 Procurement
- Purchased 20 complete school uniforms (shirt, shorts/skirt, shoes)
- Procured 200 exercise books (10 per child)
- Assembled 20 supply sets (pens, pencils, ruler, eraser, sharpener)
- Purchased 20 school bags
- Achieved cost savings through bulk uniform purchase

### 2.3 Distribution Ceremony
- Held ceremony at front of classroom
- School setting: bare cinder block walls, basic wooden desks
- All 20 children received their complete kits
- 8 volunteers coordinated the event
- Event documented with photos and records

## 4. Impact

- **Direct beneficiaries:** 20 children now equipped to attend school with proper materials
- **Institutional:** Partnership with school administration formalized for future drives
- **Programmatic:** Established repeatable process for annual scaling
- **Community:** Increased visibility of ADA's mission in Buea

## 5. Lessons Learned

1. **Bulk purchasing saves money** — Negotiating bulk rates for uniforms saved 15,000 CFA
2. **Headmaster partnership is key** — School leadership ensures accurate beneficiary identification
3. **Formalization matters** — Moving from informal (2021) to structured process improves accountability
4. **Growth potential** — Program designed to scale with additional funding in future years

## 6. Recommendations

- Increase beneficiary count in 2023 based on available funding
- Maintain headmaster partnership for beneficiary identification
- Add follow-up visit mid-year to assess kit condition
- Document process for replication in other communities

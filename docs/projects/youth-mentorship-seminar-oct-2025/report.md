## 1. Executive Summary

ADA successfully delivered its first dedicated youth mentorship seminar for boys and male adolescents on October 26–27, 2025, at Full Gospel Mission Church in Buea. The two-day program engaged 30 young males aged 8–20 in faith-based mentorship, Bible study, and life skills training. Three facilitators delivered laptop-based presentations supported by 4 ADA volunteers. The program was completed under budget at 465,000 CFA ($775) against a budget of 480,000 CFA ($800).

## 8. Challenges & Lessons Learned

| Challenge | Mitigation/Lesson |
|-----------|-------------------|
| First time targeting boys — no prior template | Adapted existing girls' program framework; worked well |
| Wide age range (8–20) | Facilitators adjusted content delivery; older youth helped younger ones |
| Two-day commitment for young participants | Refreshments and engaging presentations maintained attendance |

## 9. Recommendations

1. **Repeat quarterly** — Establish regular mentorship sessions for male youth
2. **Peer mentorship** — Pair older participants (15–20) with younger ones (8–14) for ongoing support
3. **Follow-up tracking** — Check in with participants at 3 and 6 months
4. **Expand partnership** — Explore additional church venues for broader reach
5. **Increase materials** — Provide take-home resources for continued learning

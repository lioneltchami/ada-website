## 1. Background

The Apoti Development Association (ADA) has been empowering vulnerable communities in Cameroon since 2021 through faith-based programs supporting widows, orphans, and young women. This project represents ADA's first expansion beyond its established operational areas of Buea and Bamenda into Yaoundé, the Centre Region capital.

The target beneficiaries are young women who completed secondary school but could not afford university education. They are living with relatives on temporary arrangements and lack marketable skills to achieve economic independence.

## 2. Objective

To provide intensive vocational training in hairdressing and tailoring to 10 young women, equipping them with practical skills and business knowledge to generate sustainable income.

## 3. Specific Objectives

- Train 10 young women in hairdressing and tailoring over 6 months
- Provide monthly stipends to support participants during training
- Deliver a business skills module to prepare graduates for self-employment
- Equip graduates with starter kits to begin earning income immediately
- Establish ADA's operational presence in Yaoundé

## 4. Target Beneficiaries

10 young women aged 18–30 meeting the following criteria:
- Completed secondary school
- Unable to afford university education
- Currently without marketable vocational skills
- Living in temporary arrangements with relatives in Yaoundé

## 6. Expected Outcomes

- 10 women graduate with vocational skills in hairdressing and/or tailoring
- Graduates equipped with starter kits to begin practicing their trade
- At least 80% of graduates earning income within 3 months of completion
- ADA operational framework established in Yaoundé

## 1. Background

The Back-to-School program is ADA's third annual education support initiative in Buea. Building on previous years, the 2024 edition operates at a larger scale with a formalized beneficiary identification process through community leaders and a church community hall as the distribution venue.

## 2. Objective

To provide 25 vulnerable children in Buea with complete school kits—including bags, textbooks, exercise books, stationery, and school fees contributions—enabling them to begin the 2024/2025 academic year with adequate materials.

## 3. Scope of Work

### 3.1 Beneficiary Identification
- Collaborate with community leaders to identify 25 vulnerable children
- Verify eligibility through established criteria (orphans, children of widows, economically disadvantaged)

### 3.2 Procurement
- Purchase 25 school bags, textbook sets, exercise book sets (10 per child), and stationery kits
- Arrange school fees contributions for each beneficiary

### 3.3 Distribution Event
- Secure church community hall as venue
- Organize individual distribution ceremony (each child called forward)
- Include prayer/worship session
- Provide refreshments

## 4. Team Composition

- 8 ADA volunteers responsible for logistics, procurement, coordination, and event facilitation

## 5. Expected Outcomes

- 25 children receive complete school kits
- School fees paid for all 25 beneficiaries
- Church partnership strengthened
- Individual recognition provided to each child

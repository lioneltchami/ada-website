# Project Report: Back-to-School 2024

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|

| **Organization** | Apoti Development Association (ADA) |
| **Location** | Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon |
| **Period** | September 1–30, 2024 |
| **Beneficiaries** | 25 vulnerable children |
| **Team** | 8 ADA volunteers |

## 1. Executive Summary

ADA successfully completed its third annual Back-to-School initiative in Buea, distributing complete school kits to 25 vulnerable children. The 2024 edition represented a larger scale than previous years, with a formalized identification process through community leaders and a church community hall serving as the distribution venue.

## 2. Activities Completed

### 2.1 Beneficiary Identification
- Partnered with community leaders to identify and verify 25 vulnerable children
- Formalized identification process established for future programs

### 2.2 Procurement
- Purchased 25 school bags, textbook sets, exercise books (10 per child), and stationery kits
- Organized materials into individual gift bags for each child

### 2.3 Distribution Event
- Venue: Church community hall, Buea
- Stacks of notebooks organized on tables for orderly distribution
- Each child called forward individually for personal recognition
- Gift bags with exercise books, textbooks, and supplies distributed
- Prayer and worship session held as part of the ceremony

### 2.4 School Fees
- School fees contributions of 10,000 CFA per child paid for all 25 beneficiaries

## 5. Lessons Learned

- Formalized identification through community leaders improved targeting accuracy
- Church community hall provided adequate space for organized distribution
- Individual recognition approach (calling each child forward) added dignity to the process
- Larger scale (vs. previous years) was manageable with 8 volunteers

## 6. Recommendations

- Continue scaling the program in subsequent years
- Maintain the formalized identification process
- Strengthen church partnership for future venue access
- Consider follow-up visits to track academic progress of beneficiaries

## 1. Background & Context

Orphaned children in Cameroon's South-West Region face compounding disadvantages that severely limit their access to education and basic necessities. The loss of one or both parents — whether through the ongoing Anglophone crisis, disease, or other causes — often leaves children in the care of elderly relatives, overburdened extended family members, or community institutions with limited resources. These children frequently attend school without basic supplies, eating utensils, or adequate materials for daily life, creating barriers to both learning and dignity.

In rural communities surrounding Buea, primary schools serve as critical anchors for vulnerable children, providing not only education but also structure, socialization, and — in some cases — the only consistent adult supervision a child receives. However, many orphaned children attend school without bags to carry their materials, without plates or cups for school feeding programmes, and without the basic household items that other children take for granted. The absence of these items marks them as different, contributing to social stigma and reduced self-esteem.

The Anglophone crisis, now in its sixth year, has devastated the education system in the South-West Region. Schools have been targets of violence, teachers have fled, and enrollment has dropped dramatically. Those schools that remain open — particularly in rural areas — operate with minimal resources and cannot provide supplies to students. Parents and guardians who might have purchased school materials face economic hardship from displacement, loss of livelihoods, and market disruption.

The Apoti Development Association (ADA), having completed its inaugural widow home visits in November 2021 (ADA/2021/001), identified the needs of orphaned children as a natural extension of its mission. Several of the widows visited in 2021 were caring for grandchildren who lacked basic school supplies. This project represents ADA's first school-based intervention and its first event targeting children — a significant expansion from individual home visits to a community-level distribution event. The growth of the volunteer team from 3 to 8 members reflects increasing community confidence in ADA's mission and capacity.

## 2. Project Objectives

### General Objective

To improve the material well-being and educational readiness of orphaned children attending a rural primary school in Buea, South-West Region, Cameroon, through the provision of essential school supplies and household items.

### Specific Objectives

1. **SO1:** To procure and distribute complete supply kits (school bags, plastic buckets, plates, cups, and utensils) to 20 identified orphaned children at a rural primary school by February 15, 2022.
2. **SO2:** To conduct an interactive engagement session with beneficiary children to foster a sense of community, belonging, and encouragement.
3. **SO3:** To establish ADA's first institutional partnership with a primary school as a platform for future child-focused programming.
4. **SO4:** To demonstrate ADA's expanded operational capacity through the coordination of 8 volunteers in a school-based distribution event.
5. **SO5:** To document the event and beneficiary profiles as a foundation for future child welfare programming and donor reporting.

Beneficiaries shall be identified in collaboration with school administration and must meet the following criteria:

- Enrolled at the partner primary school
- Orphaned (one or both parents deceased)
- Living with extended family, grandparents, or community caregivers
- Lacking basic school supplies (bag, writing materials)

### Indirect Beneficiaries

- Caregivers and guardians of the orphaned children (reduced financial burden for school supplies)
- School community (improved morale, demonstration of community support)
- ADA volunteer team (capacity building, team cohesion, organizational learning)

## 4. Scope of Work / Activities

The project shall undertake the following activities:

### 4.1 School Partnership & Beneficiary Identification
- Engage school administration to establish partnership and obtain permission for distribution event
- Collaborate with teachers to identify eligible orphaned children using selection criteria
- Verify beneficiary list through school records and community consultation
- Finalize list of 20 beneficiary children with basic profile information

### 4.2 Procurement of Supply Kits
- Purchase 20 colorful plastic buckets (to serve as storage containers and carrying vessels)
- Purchase 20 sets of plates, cups, and eating utensils
- Purchase 20 pink school bags suitable for primary school children
- Inspect all items for quality and completeness
- Assemble individual kits: bucket filled with plate, cup, and utensils, paired with school bag

### 4.3 Event Logistics & Preparation
- Coordinate event date and venue with school administration
- Organize volunteer team roles and responsibilities (8 volunteers)
- Arrange vehicle hire for transporting supply kits to school
- Prepare event programme including interactive engagement activities
- Print beneficiary acknowledgment forms and event documentation materials

### 4.4 Distribution Event
- Transport all supply kits to the school on event day (February 15, 2022)
- Set up distribution area within school premises
- Conduct interactive engagement session with children (songs, encouragement, group activities)
- Distribute individual supply kits to each identified beneficiary child
- Ensure each child receives complete kit and understands the contents
- Provide refreshments to children during the event

### 4.5 Documentation & Photography
- Photograph the distribution event (with school and guardian consent)
- Record beneficiary acknowledgment of items received
- Document volunteer participation and team coordination
- Capture children's reactions and engagement for organizational records

### 4.6 Post-Event Reporting
- Compile event report with photos, beneficiary data, and outcomes
- Document lessons learned and recommendations for future school-based events
- Prepare financial accountability report with receipts
- Share summary with school administration and ADA board

## 8. Implementation Approach

### Methodology

The project employs a **school-based, community-partnership, event-driven** approach:

1. **Institutional Partnership:** Unlike ADA's previous door-to-door model, this project partners with an existing institution (primary school) to identify beneficiaries and host the distribution event. This approach enables reaching more beneficiaries efficiently while building sustainable relationships with community institutions.

2. **Kit-Based Distribution:** Rather than distributing individual items, the project provides complete kits (bucket + utensils + school bag) that address multiple needs simultaneously. The bucket serves as both a carrying vessel and a storage container, maximizing utility.

3. **Interactive Engagement:** The distribution is embedded within an engagement session designed to make children feel valued and celebrated, not merely recipients of charity. Songs, encouragement, and group activities create a positive, memorable experience.

4. **Volunteer-Driven:** The expanded team of 8 volunteers enables a more ambitious event while maintaining ADA's low-overhead, community-based model. Volunteer roles are clearly defined to ensure smooth coordination.

5. **Dignity-Centred:** All children receive identical kits to avoid comparison or stigma. The event is designed to be celebratory rather than transactional, emphasizing community care over charitable handout.

## 9. Monitoring & Evaluation

### Monitoring Framework

| Level | Activity | Frequency | Responsible |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Input | Budget tracking, procurement verification, kit quality check | Weekly / pre-event | Logistics Lead |
| Process | Volunteer coordination, event preparation tracking | Daily during event week | Project Coordinator |
| Output | Distribution count, beneficiary acknowledgment forms | During event | Documentation Lead |
| Outcome | Child engagement observations, school feedback | During and after event | Event Facilitator |

### Evaluation Approach

- **Pre-event checklist:** Verification that all 20 kits are complete and transport is confirmed
- **Event-day monitoring:** Real-time tracking of distribution progress and child engagement
- **Post-event debrief:** Team meeting within 48 hours to capture observations, challenges, and lessons
- **School feedback:** Brief conversation with school administration regarding event execution and impact
- **Beneficiary observation:** Qualitative notes on children's reactions and engagement during the event

### Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if:
- All 20 identified children receive complete supply kits
- The interactive engagement session is conducted with active child participation
- The event is completed within the planned timeline and budget
- School administration expresses willingness for future collaboration
- A comprehensive event report with photos is produced within 2 weeks of the event
- At least 3 actionable recommendations for future school-based events are identified

## 12. Risks & Assumptions

### Assumptions

- School administration will cooperate in identifying eligible orphaned children and hosting the event
- Identified children will be present at school on the distribution day
- Market prices for buckets, utensils, and school bags will remain within budgeted estimates
- All 8 volunteers will be available on the event day
- Road and transport conditions will permit vehicle access to the school
- Weather conditions will allow an outdoor or semi-outdoor event
- Guardians/caregivers will consent to their children's participation and photography

### Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Security deterioration due to Anglophone crisis restricts movement or school closure | Medium | High | Monitor security situation; identify backup date; maintain communication with school on operational status |
| Fewer than 20 eligible children identified at the school | Low | Medium | Expand identification to neighbouring schools or community referrals; adjust kit quantities accordingly |
| Market price increases exceed budget for supply items | Low | Medium | Procure early in project cycle; seek wholesale pricing; adjust item specifications within budget |
| Volunteer attrition on event day | Low | Medium | Confirm attendance 48 hours before; identify 2 standby volunteers; assign backup roles |
| Adverse weather on distribution day | Medium | Low | Identify indoor backup venue at school; have flexible date option |
| Children absent on distribution day | Low | Low | Coordinate with school to ensure attendance; hold reserved kits for absent children to collect later |
| Transport vehicle unavailable on event day | Low | Medium | Confirm booking 72 hours before; identify alternative vehicle; consider splitting transport across two trips |

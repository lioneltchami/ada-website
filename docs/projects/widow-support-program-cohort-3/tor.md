## 1. Background

ADA has supported widows through vocational training and food assistance since 2022. Cohort 1 (Bamenda, 2022) graduated 12 of 15 widows who are now earning independent income. Cohort 2 consolidated the Bamenda model. Cohort 3 expands operations to Douala while continuing Bamenda activities, reaching 20 widows across two cities simultaneously.

Widows are identified through church partner networks in both cities and selected based on vulnerability criteria including lack of income, dependent children, and absence of family support.

## 2. Objectives

1. Provide monthly food support to 20 widows for 6 months to stabilize household nutrition.
2. Deliver vocational training (tailoring and hairdressing) to equip widows with marketable skills.
3. Supply starter kits enabling graduates to launch micro-enterprises.
4. Expand the proven Bamenda model to Douala, establishing ADA's multi-city operational capacity.

## 6. Expected Outcomes

- 20 widows receive consistent food support for 6 months.
- 20 widows complete vocational training in tailoring or hairdressing.
- 20 widows receive starter kits to begin income-generating activities.
- ADA establishes operational presence in Douala.
- At least 80% of graduates earning independent income within 6 months of completion (based on Cohort 1 results).

## 7. Monitoring & Evaluation

- Monthly attendance and distribution records.
- Skills assessment at mid-point and end of training.
- Post-program income tracking (3 and 6 months after graduation).
- Coordinator reports submitted monthly to ADA leadership.

## 8. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Beneficiary dropout | Regular check-ins, stipend incentive |
| Instructor unavailability | Backup instructor identified per city |
| Logistics between 2 cities | Dedicated coordinators per location |
| Currency fluctuation | Budget in CFA with contingency buffer |
